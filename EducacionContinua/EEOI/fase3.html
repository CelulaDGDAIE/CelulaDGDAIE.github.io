Generating ideas

Reflect on the following questions:

Have you ever asked yourself the following questions?

  1. What should you do when having oral presentations?
  2. What are some basic skills for effective oral presentations?
  3. What skills must you develop in order to conduct effective meetings?
  4. What do you need to know for holding a successful interview? 

Nowadays getting a high-flying job is becoming one of the most essential objectives for everybody and specially for people who have recently finished their studies, however how can they make a skilled impression at an interview? And once they get the job what do they  need to consider to present their results or conduct meetings at work? In this module, you can study and learn some significant facts to consider for those situations in the work context.

3.2.1. Activity: Vocabulary activity about meetings

In this module, you  are going to learn about meetings, but first let's review some vocabulary related to this main topic. It's important to check meaning because a pair of them are false cognates. They don't have the meaning they seem to have, at least in this context. Guess what words are we talking about?

Vocabulary:

Meet.

Meeting.

Hold.

Outcome.

Agenda.

Minute.

Attendee.

Observe that the two words that are false cognates are:

Agenda: In this context agenda means a list of topics to be discussed at a formal meeting.

Minutes: It means a summarized record of the topics discussed at a meeting.

Project 3:

Video of a presentation of a topic or a job interview

Description:

Participants can choose to make a video of a presentation of a topic or a job interview mastering what they studied in this module.

Evaluation criteria:

Participants’ script must:

  1. Have clarity of purpose and thought.
  2. Convey accurate, relevant and complete information.
  3. Have short, concise and clear ideas.
  4. Have correct grammar and appropriate vocabulary

Participants’ video must:

  1. Run well (sound and image).
  2. Last between 2 or 3 minutes.
  3. Be recorded in mp4 format.

Guidelines:

Short oral presentation

  1. Participants choose a common topic from their area (one they could present soon if they work) or a topic they may present when working in a company.
  2. Participants write their script.
  3. Participants choose, organize and use their visual aids when presenting their topic.
  4. Participants, when ready, make a video of their oral presentation(taking into account all the aspects for successful oral presentations).
  5. Participantes record their video as if they were in front of their colleagues.
  6. It can be between 2 to 3 minutes long in an mp4 format.
  7. Participants upload or share the link of their video.

Job interview

Participants choose five of the following examples and prepare their answers:

  1. Tell me about yourself. 
  2. What is your greatest strength?
  3. Can you describe a situation in your past where you learned from a mistake?
  4. What is the most difficult (job) situation you have ever faced?
  5. Is there anything you would like to improve about yourself?
  6. What is the most important thing you are looking for in a job?
  7. What are your career goals?
  8. What motivates you?
  9. Why would you like to work for us?
  10. Why should I hire you?

Before recording your video

  1. Participants rehearse their answers, paying attention to verbal communication and body language.
  2. They may also pay attention to the pieces of advice given in module 3 for interviews.
  3. When ready, participants make a video simulating they are in a job interview.

They upload their video or share the link
